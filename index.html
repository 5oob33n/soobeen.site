SOOBEEN WOO

Soobeen Woo captures the unseen, shaping it into form—then dismantles it to let it vanish again. Breath, vibration, warmth, and time pass through her hands, becoming sound, light, fracture, and the texture of clay and glass.

Her work resists the human gaze, experimenting with ways in which existence can refuse to be perceived. At the threshold between material and immaterial, she questions the ethics of sensing and explores how disappearance can reveal the essence of being.
